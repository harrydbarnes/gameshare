PS5 Game Sharing Setup Guide

Enter Player Names

Instructions for Player 1 (Game Owner)

These are the steps Player 1 (the person who owns the games) needs to follow. Crucially, these steps are performed on Player 2's PS5 console. Player 1 will need their PSN login credentials.

  1. On Player 2's PS5, go to Settings (the gear icon in the top right).
  2. Select Users and Accounts.
  3. Select Other.
  4. Select Console Sharing and Offline Play.
  5. If it says "Disable" for this PS5, it means it's already enabled, likely for another account on Player 2's console. If Player 2 is already game sharing with someone else, or if their own account has this enabled, Player 1 cannot enable it for their account simultaneously on this console. One PS5 can only have "Console Sharing and Offline Play" enabled for *one* account at a time. Assuming it's not enabled, or Player 2 is okay with disabling it for their own account (or another account), proceed.
  6. Select Enable. If it says "Don't Disable", then Player 1's account is already set as the primary on a *different* PS5. Player 1 can only have one PS5 designated for "Console Sharing and Offline Play". If Player 1 wants to proceed, they would need to disable it on their other PS5 first.
  7. Once "Console Sharing and Offline Play" is Enabled for Player 1's account on Player 2's PS5, Player 1 should log out of their account on Player 2's PS5. (Go to Settings > Users and Accounts > Other > Sign Out). It's good practice for security.

Summary for Player 1: Log into your PSN account on Player 2's PS5, enable "Console Sharing and Offline Play" for your account on that console, then log out.

Instructions for Player 2 (Game Receiver)

After Player 1 has completed the steps above on Player 2's PS5, Player 2 can now access Player 1's games.

  1. Make sure Player 2 is logged into their own PSN account on their PS5.
  2. Go to the Game Library (usually at the far right of the games row on the home screen).
  3. In the Game Library, navigate to the "Your Collection" tab. Player 2 should now see all of Player 1's digital PS5 and PS4 games listed alongside their own.
  4. Player 2 can download and play any of Player 1's games.

Summary for Player 2: Simply log into your own account on your PS5, and Player 1's games will appear in your library, ready to download and play.

Important Considerations & Risks

  • Trust is Essential: Player 1 is giving Player 2 access to their entire library of games. More importantly, to enable this, Player 1 has to log into their PSN account on Player 2's console. Only do this with someone you trust implicitly. Player 1 should ideally log out of their account on Player 2's console after setup, but their account details will have been entered. Consider enabling 2-Step Verification on Player 1's account for added security.
  • Primary Console Designation: Player 1's account can only have "Console Sharing and Offline Play" enabled on ONE PS5 console at a time. By enabling it on Player 2's PS5, Player 1's own PS5 (if they have one) is no longer their primary console for that account. This has implications for Player 1 (see below).
  • Digital Games Only: Game sharing only works for digital games purchased from the PlayStation Store. It does not work for disc-based games.
  • Account Bans (Low Risk but Possible): While generally accepted, sharing account details is technically against PlayStation's Terms of Service. The risk of an account ban is low, especially for sharing between close friends or family, but it's not zero.
  • DLC and Add-ons: DLC and add-ons purchased by Player 1 are generally shared along with the base game.

Post-Setup Information

  • Player 1's Offline Play: Since Player 1's PS5 is no longer their primary console (because Player 2's PS5 is now designated for "Console Sharing and Offline Play" for Player 1's account), Player 1 must be connected to the internet on their own PS5 to play their own digital games. If their internet goes down, they will lose access to their digital titles until the connection is restored. This is the main trade-off for Player 1.
  • Player 2's Offline Play: Player 2 can play Player 1's shared digital games on their PS5 (where "Console Sharing and Offline Play" is enabled for Player 1's account) offline without any issues. They can also play their own digital games offline.
  • Simultaneous Online Play (Different Accounts, Different Games): Yes. Player 1 can be signed into their account on their PS5 playing Game A, and Player 2 can be signed into their own account on their PS5 playing Game B (one of Player 1's shared games or their own).
  • Simultaneous Online Play (Different Accounts, Same Shared Game): Generally, yes. Player 1 can play a game they own, and Player 2 can play that same game via game sharing simultaneously, as long as they are on their respective accounts. Some games might have specific restrictions, but it usually works.
  • Can both players be signed into Player 1's account at the same time on different consoles? No. If Player 1 signs into their account on their PS5 while Player 2 (or anyone) is signed into Player 1's account on Player 2's PS5, the person who was signed in first will usually be kicked out. For game sharing to work as intended, Player 2 plays on THEIR OWN account.
  • What if Player 1 changes their Primary PS5 activation? If Player 1 later disables "Console Sharing and Offline Play" on Player 2's PS5 (e.g., by enabling it on their own PS5 again or a new PS5), Player 2 will immediately lose access to Player 1's shared games. The games will appear with a lock icon.
  • Maintaining Access: For Player 2 to maintain access, Player 1's account must keep "Console Sharing and Offline Play" enabled on Player 2's PS5.
  • Subscription Services (PlayStation Plus): Benefits like online multiplayer access from PS Plus are tied to the account that has the subscription. If Player 1 has PS Plus, Player 2 (on the console where Player 1 has "Console Sharing and Offline Play" enabled) can play Player 1's games online using Player 2's own account. Monthly PS Plus games claimed by Player 1 will also be available to Player 2 as long as Player 1's PS Plus subscription is active and game sharing is set up.

Game sharing requires a high degree of trust. Only share with individuals you know well and trust with your account information. Understand the implications for offline play for the game owner (Player 1).
